Why Smart Agriculture?

Soil fertility is the ability of soil to sustain plant growth and optimize crop yield. The soil is considered infertile if there is deficiency or overdose of nutrients available for plant growth. Both less and over- fertilization can lead to sudden plant growth with an insufficient root system to supply adequate water and nutrients to the plant. Poor root structure reduces the number of flowers and fruit production, and can result in plant growth spurts that won't be supported or sustained. This reduces crop yield and hence the need to monitor and control the nutrients available for plant growth

How will Smart Agriculture help you?

SA will enable farmers to measure nutrients on their soil/hydroponics solutions before taking any action and help farmers know the right time to apply different fertilizers to ensure great yields. Farmers will also be able to monitor and control these nutrients remotely via mobile phones or PC systems.
